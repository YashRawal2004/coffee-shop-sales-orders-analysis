## üìå Project Overview
**Note: This is a practice project developed for educational purposes to demonstrate data analysis and Power BI skills.**

This project involves analyzing sales transaction data for a coffee shop to reveal key insights regarding customer purchase behavior, product performance, and operational trends. The interactive Power BI dashboard allows stakeholders to visualize cumulative sales, track Month-over-Month (MoM) growth, and identify peak operational hours.

The goal of this analysis is to provide data-driven recommendations to optimize staffing, inventory management, and marketing strategies.

---

## üìä Dashboard Features

The dashboard consists of three main analytical views designed to answer specific business questions:

### 1. Sales Overview
* **KPI Cards:** High-level metrics for Total Sales ($698.81K), Total Orders (149K), and Total Quantity Sold.
* **Trend Analysis:**
    * **Cumulative Sales:** Tracking revenue progression throughout the year (Jan‚ÄìJun).
    * **MoM Growth:** Identifying monthly percentage growth or decline.
    * **Sales/Orders by Month:** Line charts visualizing the seasonal trajectory of the business.

### 2. Product Analysis
* **Top 5 Products:** Bar charts ranking Product Categories (e.g., Coffee, Tea, Bakery) and Product Types (e.g., Barista Espresso, Brewed Chai Tea) by both **Sales Volume** and **Total Revenue**.
* **Drill-down Capability:** Analysis of high-margin vs. high-volume items.

### 3. Customer Traffic & Operations
* **Heatmaps:** A Day/Hour matrix (Monday‚ÄìSunday vs. Opening Hours) to pinpoint exact peak traffic times.
* **Weekday vs. Weekend Split:** A donut chart visualizing the revenue distribution between workdays and weekends.
* **Daily Trends:** Line charts showing daily fluctuations in orders and sales.

## üîç Key Insights

Based on the analysis of the data from January through June, the following insights were generated:

### üìà Sales Trends
* **Strong Spring Momentum:** The business showed a significant upward trajectory starting in March (**~30% MoM growth**), peaking in **May** with over **$160K** in monthly revenue.
* **February Dip:** February was the only month to experience negative growth (-7%), suggesting a seasonal lull or post-holiday drop-off.
* **Average Order Value:** With ~$699k in revenue across 149k transactions, the Average Order Value (AOV) sits at approximately **$4.69**.

### ‚òï Product Performance
* **Volume vs. Value:**
    * **Most Popular (Volume):** *Brewed Chai Tea* is the most frequently ordered item (>15k orders), driving foot traffic.
    * **Most Profitable (Value):** *Barista Espresso* generates the highest revenue (>$90k), indicating a higher price point or better margins than tea.
* **Category Dominance:** **Coffee** is the clear leader, accounting for the bulk of sales ($270k+).
* **Food Attachment:** **Bakery** items rank 3rd in orders (~22k), showing a solid attachment rate, though revenue contribution is lower ($80k) due to lower unit prices.

### ‚è≥ Operational Patterns
* **The "Commuter Economy":** The shop is heavily reliant on weekday traffic. In March, **74.23%** of orders occurred on Weekdays, with only **25.77%** on Weekends.
* **Golden Hours:** Heatmaps reveal consistently high traffic between **8:00 AM and 10:00 AM** on Weekdays (Mon‚ÄìFri). Traffic drops off significantly after 4:00 PM.
